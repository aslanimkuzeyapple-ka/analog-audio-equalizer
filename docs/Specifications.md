** Project Specifications

 Objective
Design and build a three-band analog audio equalizer to demonstrate practical analog circuit design using operational amplifiers and active filters. The project focuses on applying circuit analysis, filter design, simulation, hardware implementation, PCB design, and experimental validation to strengthen analog engineering skills and build a professional engineering portfolio.

 Input Signal
The equalizer will accept a low-level analog audio signal through a 3.5 mm AUX input. The input should be compatible with common audio sources such as smartphones, laptops, and laboratory function generators.

 Output Signal
The equalizer will provide a low-level analog audio output through a 3.5 mm AUX connector. The output should be compatible with powered speakers, amplifiers, headphones (through an external amplifier if required), oscilloscopes, and other audio measurement equipment.

 Number of Bands
Three independently adjustable frequency bands:
- Bass
- Mid
- Treble

 Frequency Bands
The equalizer will independently control three audio frequency regions based on the standard audible spectrum:

- Bass: 20 Hz – 250 Hz
- Mid: 250 Hz – 4 kHz
- Treble: 4 kHz – 20 kHz


 Gain Range
Gain Range: 12dB continuously adjustable for each band.
Justification: Providing 12dB offers distinct acoustic modification without driving the operational amplifiers into saturation (clipping), preserving signal integrity and avoiding audio distortion.
